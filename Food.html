Pulihora

  • A Tangy Rice Specialty: Pulihora, also known as tamarind or puliyodarai rice, features a vibrant tangy flavor from tamarind pulp, balanced with saltiness and a hint of spice.
  • Traditional South Indian Dish: Widely enjoyed across Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu—it's a staple in temple offerings, lunchboxes, and travel meals.
  • Key Ingredients: It’s made using rice, tamarind pulp, turmeric, spices (mustard seeds, dried chilies, curry leaves), peanuts or chana dal, and sometimes chopped green chilies.
  • Festive and Ritual Use: Pulihora is a must-offering in many festivals and religious ceremonies in Andhra Pradesh—symbolizing purity and tradition in prasadam and naivedyam.
  • Easy to Store & Carry: Aromatic and non-perishable for hours, it’s ideal for picnics, travel, and tiffins—often kept fresh with a drizzle of gingelly (sesame) oil and a sprinkle of fresh coriander.

Natu Kodi Pulusu Ragi Sangati

  • Authentic Village Dish: Natu Kodi Pulusu is a spicy curry made with country chicken (native, free-range chicken), known for its rich flavor and nutritional value. It is a staple in many rural households of Andhra Pradesh.
  • Healthy Combination: It is commonly served with Ragi Sangati, a nutritious ball made from finger millet (ragi) and rice, rich in calcium, fiber, and iron.
  • Flavorful and Spicy: The curry is cooked with traditional spices, onions, tomatoes, and sometimes coconut or tamarind, giving it a deep, spicy, and tangy taste.
  • Power-packed Meal: This combination is both energy-boosting and filling, often consumed by farmers and laborers for strength and stamina.
  • Cultural Specialty: Widely served during family gatherings, festivals, and village feasts, it represents the rich culinary heritage of Rayalaseema and southern Andhra regions.

Royyala Vepudu

  • Spicy Andhra Style: Royyala Vepudu is a fiery prawn fry dish made in traditional Andhra style, known for its bold flavors and spicy masala.
  • Simple Ingredients: It is prepared using prawns, onions, garlic, ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, and a blend of Andhra-style spices like red chili powder and garam masala.
  • Quick & Flavorful: The dish is quick to cook and delivers intense flavor, with prawns absorbing the spices perfectly due to their soft texture.
  • Popular Side Dish: Royyala Vepudu is commonly served as a side dish with plain rice, dal, or rasam, and is also enjoyed as a starter.
  • Loved in Coastal Andhra: It’s especially popular in coastal districts of Andhra Pradesh, where fresh seafood is a regular part of daily meals.

Punugulu

  • Popular South Indian Snack: Punugulu is a crispy, deep-fried snack commonly made in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana using leftover dosa or idli batter.
  • Simple Ingredients: The batter is mixed with onions, green chilies, curry leaves, cumin, and sometimes rice flour or sooji for added crispiness.
  • Crispy Outside, Soft Inside: Punugulu are golden brown and crunchy on the outside, while remaining soft and fluffy inside, making them a perfect evening snack.
  • Served with Chutneys: They are typically served hot with spicy peanut chutney, coconut chutney, or ginger chutney for extra flavor.
  • Street Food Favorite: Punugulu is a popular street food in Andhra towns and cities, especially during rainy evenings, loved by both kids and adults.

Pesarattu

  • Healthy Andhra Breakfast: Pesarattu is a nutritious dosa-like dish made from green gram (moong dal), popular in Andhra Pradesh.
  • Protein-Rich: It is rich in protein, fiber, and iron, making it a healthy option for breakfast or a light meal, especially for vegetarians.
  • No Fermentation Needed: Unlike regular dosa, pesarattu batter does not require fermentation—just soak, grind, and cook.
  • Often Served with Upma: A popular combo in Andhra cuisine is Pesarattu with Upma, known as MLA Pesarattu, served with ginger or coconut chutney.
  • Quick and Tasty: With added green chilies, ginger, onions, and cumin, pesarattu is flavorful, crispy, and easy to prepare.

Uggani Bajji

  • Popular Rayalaseema Breakfast: Uggani Bajji is a beloved breakfast and snack combination in the Rayalaseema and Kurnool regions of Andhra Pradesh.
  • Made with Puffed Rice: Uggani is prepared using puffed rice (maramaralu) mixed with turmeric, green chilies, onions, and mustard seeds for a light, flavorful dish.
  • Served with Mirchi Bajji: It is traditionally served with spicy mirchi bajji (deep-fried green chili fritters), creating a perfect balance of mild and spicy flavors.
  • Quick and Easy: The dish is easy to prepare, requires no soaking or grinding, and is ideal for a quick, filling meal.
  • Street Food Favorite: Uggani Bajji is also a popular street food in southern Andhra towns, loved for its unique taste and satisfying combination.

Ulava Charu

  • Traditional Andhra Dish: Ulava Charu is a popular and traditional soup-like dish from Andhra Pradesh, especially famous in the Krishna and Guntur regions.
  • Made from Horse Gram: The dish is prepared using ulavalu (horse gram), which is soaked, boiled, and slow-cooked to extract a rich, flavorful broth.
  • Rich and Nutritious: Horse gram is packed with protein, iron, and dietary fiber, making Ulava Charu a healthy and energy-boosting food.
  • Served with Rice and Cream: Ulava Charu is usually served with hot steamed rice, topped with a spoon of fresh cream or butter for added richness.
  • Winter Favorite: Due to its warming and nourishing qualities, Ulava Charu is especially enjoyed during winter and is also served at festive meals and weddings in Andhra.

Gutti Vankaya

  • Popular Andhra Dish: Gutti Vankaya is a traditional and much-loved curry in Andhra Pradesh, made using small brinjals (eggplants) stuffed with a spicy masala.
  • Flavorful Stuffing: The stuffing typically includes a blend of roasted peanuts, sesame seeds, coconut, tamarind, and spices, giving the dish a rich, tangy, and nutty flavor.
  • Slow-Cooked Perfection: The brinjals are carefully cooked in the masala until they become soft and flavorful, absorbing all the spices.
  • Best with Rice or Rotis: Gutti Vankaya pairs perfectly with steamed rice, especially with ghee, and also goes well with rotis or jowar roti.
  • Festive and Special Dish: It is often prepared for festivals, weddings, and special occasions in Andhra households, showcasing the region's culinary richness.
