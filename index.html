Amaravati – The People’s Capital of Andhra Pradesh

Amaravati is a historic and culturally rich city located on the banks of the Krishna River in the Guntur district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It has been chosen as the legislative capital of Andhra Pradesh after the bifurcation of the state in 2014. The city is envisioned as a modern, well-planned urban hub, blending heritage with futuristic development.Historically, Amaravati was the capital of the Satavahana dynasty around the 3rd century BCE and is famous for the Amaravati Stupa, a significant Buddhist monument. The city holds great spiritual importance for Buddhists and attracts many pilgrims from around the world.The name "Amaravati" means "Abode of Immortals," and it reflects the city’s deep spiritual roots. It is also home to the ancient Amareswara Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, which draws devotees throughout the year.The current development of Amaravati is focused on making it a green and sustainable city with world-class infrastructure, including administrative buildings, educational institutions, and residential areas. Although development has faced some political and financial challenges, Amaravati remains a symbol of ambition and growth for the people of Andhra Pradesh. Its unique blend of history, culture, and modern vision makes it a city of great promise.
